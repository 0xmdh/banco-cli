# Rootstock Institutional CLI Agent - Messaging Framework

## Strategic Positioning

**Core message:** From institutional intent to funded vault position.

This proposal positions a Rootstock CLI Agent as a conversion and lifecycle layer for institutional capital, not as another yield venue. The value is measured in funnel efficiency, time-to-deposit, and repeat allocations into Rootstock-linked vaults.

## Problem Framing (What Rootstock Cares About)

- Institutional leads drop before first funding because onboarding is fragmented across legal, custody, bridge, and execution steps.
- Allocation teams need confidence signals before moving size: controls, reporting, policy boundaries, and operational clarity.
- Post-deposit inactivity reduces expansion potential because treasury teams do not receive structured prompts for reallocation or top-ups.
- Conversations around APY alone are not enough; Rootstock needs a growth system that turns interest into funded positions and retained AUM.

## Value Proposition

The Rootstock Institutional CLI Agent provides a guided, policy-aware journey from qualification to first funded position and ongoing portfolio activity.

It helps Rootstock:

1. Convert more qualified institutional leads.
2. Reduce onboarding friction and time-to-first-deposit.
3. Increase second deposits and ongoing allocation frequency.
4. Create auditable, institutional-grade interaction records.

## Product Narrative Pillars

### 1) Conversion Engine

The agent qualifies intent, answers diligence questions, and guides teams to actionable next steps instead of passive documentation-driven drop-off.

### 2) Operational Confidence

The agent enforces guardrails (policy checks, role-aware prompts, confirmation gates) before any capital movement workflow.

### 3) Lifecycle Expansion

The same interface supports monitoring and proactive nudges (threshold-based alerts, treasury reminders, re-deposit opportunities) to increase active AUM over time.

### 4) Bitcoin-Aligned Trust Layer

The commercial story stays anchored in Rootstock strengths: Bitcoin security assumptions, PowPeg model, on-chain visibility, and institutional ecosystem integrations.

## Differentiation Against Generic "AI Assistant" Claims

- Not a chatbot wrapper: it is a funnel and allocation workflow layer.
- Not APY marketing: it is conversion + activation + retention infrastructure.
- Not retail UX: it is institutional-first orchestration with controls and auditability.

## KPI Framework (Messaging-Level)

- Lead -> Qualified institutional opportunity rate.
- Qualified -> Onboarded completion rate.
- Onboarded -> First funded vault position rate.
- First deposit -> Second deposit conversion within target period.
- Net active institutional AUM influenced by the agent journey.

## Objection Handling (Meeting-Ready)

### "Liquidity/yield may happen outside Rootstock rails"

Response: The proposal is focused on capital onboarding and lifecycle growth into Rootstock-linked products. Even when strategy layers are external, the conversion and treasury operating flow can still expand Rootstock-attributed activity.

### "Institutional users need strict controls"

Response: The CLI Agent is designed around policy checks, staged confirmations, role-aware actions, and audit trails as first-class requirements.

### "Why not just improve docs and sales playbooks?"

Response: Docs and playbooks are static. The agent is an interactive operating surface that drives decisions in context, captures intent, and moves users to next-step actions faster.

## Recommended Tone for Landing

- Business outcome first.
- Institutional and risk-aware.
- Specific on funnel outcomes.
- Conservative on technical claims.
- Clear CTA toward strategy/pilot conversation.
